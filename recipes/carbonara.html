Carbonara Recipe

Description:

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Roman dish known for its rich, creamy texture and savory flavor. Despite its luxurious taste, it is incredibly simple to make, relying on just a few high-quality ingredients: crisp guanciale or pancetta, fresh egg yolks, sharp Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous amount of freshly cracked black pepper.

Ingredients

  • Pasta: Spaghetti or Rigatoni.
  • Guanciale: Cured pork jowl.
  • Egg Yolks: Fresh eggs.
  • Pecorino Romano: A sharp, salty sheep's milk cheese.
  • Black Pepper: Freshly cracked and toasted in the pan.

Steps

  1. Crisp the Pork: Cook chopped guanciale (or bacon) in a pan over medium heat until crispy, then turn off the heat.
  2. Whisk the Base: Whisk 3 egg yolks, 1 whole egg, and a handful of grated Pecorino Romano cheese in a bowl with plenty of black pepper.
  3. Boil Pasta: Cook your pasta in salted boiling water until al dente, making sure to save 1 cup of starchy pasta water before draining.
  4. Combine Hot Pasta: Toss the hot, drained pasta directly into the pan with the warm pork and its rendered fat (off the heat).
  5. Create Sauce: Pour in the egg and cheese mixture and toss vigorously. Splash in a little saved pasta water to make it smooth, creamy, and velvety.
