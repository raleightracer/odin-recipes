Leche Flan Recipe

Description

Leche Flan is a beloved Filipino dessert consisting of a rich, velvety custard topped with a layer of sweet, golden caramel. Derived from the Spanish flan, this version is distinctly richer and denser because it is made primarily with egg yolks and a combination of condensed and evaporated milk, rather than fresh whole milk.

Ingredients

  • Egg Yolks: 10 large egg yolks (discard or save the whites for a different recipe) to give it that signature ultra-rich density.
  • Condensed Milk: 1 can (14 oz) to provide sweetness and a smooth, thick body.
  • Evaporated Milk: 1 can (12 oz) to balance the sweetness while keeping the custard creamy.
  • Sugar: 1/2 cup of granulated white sugar specifically used to melt down and create the amber caramel base.
  • Vanilla Extract: 1 teaspoon to enhance the rich aroma and mask the scent of the egg yolks.

Steps

  1. Caramelize the Sugar: Place your metal mold (llanera) directly on the stovetop over low heat, add sugar, and heat until it melts into a smooth, golden-brown liquid. Swirl to coat the bottom, then turn off heat to let it harden.
  2. Mix the Custard: In a large bowl, gently whisk the egg yolks, condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla together. **Crucial rule:** Whisk slowly in one direction so you do not create tiny air bubbles.
  3. Strain the Mixture: Pour the liquid mixture through a fine-mesh strainer (or cheesecloth) directly into your sugar-lined mold. Straining removes any unblended egg pieces, ensuring a completely silky finish.
  4. Cover and Steam: Cover the mold tightly with aluminum foil to prevent steam water from dripping inside. Place it in a steamer over medium-low heat and steam gently for about 30 to 35 minutes until the center is set but slightly jiggly.
  5. Chill and Flip: Let it cool down to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours. To serve, run a thin knife carefully around the inside edge of the mold, place a serving plate on top, and quickly flip it over!
