Adobo Recipe

Description

Adobong Baboy is a quintessential Filipino dish made by simmering tender chunks of pork belly in a flavorful marinade of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. It is widely considered the unofficial national dish of the Philippines because of its deep, comforting flavors and its incredible shelf-life.

Ingredients

  • Pork Belly: Cut into bite-sized cubes (pork shoulder works too, but belly gives the best flavor).
  • Garlic: A generous amount of garlic, lightly crushed or minced.
  • Soy Sauce: Provides the deep savory, salty foundation and dark color.
  • Vinegar: White cane vinegar is traditional, providing the essential tangy punch.
  • Bay Leaves & Peppercorns: Dried bay leaves and whole black peppercorns for that distinct herbal aroma and mild heat.

Steps

  1. Sear the Pork: Heat a pot over medium-high heat and sear the pork pieces until they are slightly browned and start rendering their fat.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Toss in the crushed garlic, whole peppercorns, and bay leaves, stirring for about a minute until the garlic becomes fragrant.
  3. Simmer Liquid: Pour in the soy sauce and a splash of water. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 20-30 minutes until the pork is nice and tender.
  4. Add Vinegar: Pour in the vinegar. **Crucial rule:** Let it boil uncovered for 3 to 5 minutes *without stirring* so the harsh alcohol taste cooks off.
  5. Reduce the Sauce: Stir gently, lower the heat, and let it simmer for a few more minutes until the sauce thickens up and coats the pork beautifully. Serve over warm rice!
